## A. Identification variable: d_t (the directed price)

- **[F1] d_t is predetermined and falls sharply mid-2023.** `outputs/descriptives/Gate0_dt_table.csv`. Operational d_t = reconstructed trailing-365-day 90th percentile of SA spot (`dt_real`). Pre-spike 2021: ~$66–101/MWh. Plateau 2022Q3–2023Q1: $347–350/MWh (202208 peak $349.6). Window-exit decline: 202306 $315.9 → 202307 $261.3 → 202309 $220.1 → 202312 $179.9. 2024 trough $160–225 (202402 $160.2). **Peak-to-trough −54%** ($349.6 → $160.2). The trailing-window construction makes the drop date computable from price history alone; the 2022 prices driving it predate 2023 bidding. **Rules in:** a large, predetermined "prize" shock. **Rules out:** the drop being a contemporaneous 2023 demand/supply shock.

- **[F2] Reconstructed d_t validated against realised compensation.** Same file. Corr(`dt_real`, realised DCP/DQ `dt_recon`) = **0.984** over 35 overlap months (mean abs diff $10.6); **0.997** over the 24 complete-trailing-window months (mad $7.6). **Rules in:** the d_t series is the actual compensation reference price, not a proxy. **Caveat:** spike-month level gaps (e.g. 202208 realised $378 vs reconstructed $350) reflect aggregation across issue dates and June-2022 APC treatment — note in a footnote.

- **[F3] No cost-side break at the d_t exit (SRMC is flat through the drop).** `outputs/descriptives_v3/GateA_srmc_params.csv`, `GateA_srmc.png`. Unit SRMC_marginal ($/MWh, gas-price driven): TORRB ~$104, PPCCGT ~$79, OSB-AG ~$87, MINTARO ~$114, BARKIPS1 ~$89, DRYCGT ~$120, QPS5 ~$89 (202201 levels; vary with STTM gas price). No structural break in SRMC at 202307. **Rules out:** the d_t drop coinciding with a marginal-cost shock — the variation is in the prize, not in cost.

- **[F3a] Offers do not reveal a gas-indexed cost on EITHER margin — the engineering SRMC is the maintained cost measure.** `outputs/descriptives_v3/GateA_revealed_cost{,_qty}.csv`, `.png`; `02_cost/gate_a_revealed_cost{,_qty}.R`. The revealed-cost anchor of `srmc_inputs_heatrate_vom.md` §7 fails on both the price and the quantity margin, tested over competitive intervals only (undirected, non-pivotal, system not short), so the failure is not a circularity artefact. **Price margin** (low offer band on gas, n=9,295 competitive unit-days): pooled within-unit slope (implied heat rate) = **0.22 GJ/MWh** (t 3.6, within-R² **0.003**) vs engineering 7–11; per-unit implied HRs near-zero, wrong-signed (PPCCGT −1.61, R² 0.36; MINTARO/Dry Creek ≈ −0.6) or implausible (QPS5 3.61); implied "VOM" $52–143 (true $2–16). **Quantity margin** (competitive unit-months, n=432): the price to clear the low tranche (`p_at_25`) has pooled slope on gas = **−110.7** (t −5.8) — *wrong-signed and huge*, the low tranche gets cheaper as gas rises; the share of offered capacity priced ≤ $150 has pooled slope = **−0.0002** (t −0.23, within-R² 0.0002) — dead flat, e.g. Torrens B parks ~26% below $150 whether gas is $10 or $30/GJ (SRMC $110 vs $300), where cost bidding requires that share to collapse as gas rises. Units run a **fixed base tranche at fixed low ladder prices and dump the rest near the market price cap**, invariant to fuel cost. **Rules in:** offers are not cost-reflective on either margin even on competitive intervals; conduct runs through quantity rebids + high bands [F16]. **Rules out:** using revealed cost to override the engineering SRMC or to fill the BARKIPS1 heat-rate / TORRB VOM gaps — those proxies stand, flagged.

## B. The prize is real and scales with d_t

- **[F4] d_t exceeds SRMC in 33–34 of 35 months for every unit; margins are large.** `GateA_srmc_margin_summary.csv`. Pre-exit mean (d_t − SRMC_marginal): PPCCGT $147.9, BARKIPS1 $133.4, OSB-AG $133.0, QPS5 $132.0, TORRB $102.1, MINTARO $91.5, DRYCGT $77.6. Post-exit: still positive but lower (PPCCGT $108.6, TORRB $78.3, DRYCGT $60.0). **Rules in:** being directed on is a rent for the entire sample. **Rules out:** the rent ever vanishing — even post-exit the directed price clears marginal cost.

- **[F5] Direction rent rises monotonically in d_t.** `Cut2_rent_table.csv`, `Cut2_rent_distribution.png`. Net rent (d_t − spot) per directed 5-min interval, by d_t tercile × instruction. Synchronise: Low $126.3 (96.4% positive, n=56,081) → Mid $195.0 (97.4%, 32,826) → High $268.7 (98.0%, 40,176). Remain: Low $134.1 → Mid $170.9 → High $274.7. High−Low gap ≈ **$142/MWh** (Synchronise). **Rules in:** the prize scales with d_t and is positive >94% of directed intervals always. This is the core incentive gradient.

## C. Withholding behaviour and its response to d_t

- **[F6] Withholding is the chronic default, not a sporadic response.** `Cut4_mechanism.csv`, `Cut4_mechanism.png`. Over 190 Synchronise event-starts, the day before the event: share with top price band (PB10) above SRMC = **100%** in all three d_t terciles; mean BANDAVAIL10/MAXAVAIL = 99.7 / 99.9 / 100%; share with PB10 within 5% of the market price cap = 100% in all terciles (n = 53 / 48 / 49). **Rules in:** SA synchronous units sit with ~all capacity in the top band priced above SRMC and near the cap, essentially always. **Rules out:** detecting d_t-responsiveness from any binary/near-saturated "is capacity withheld" metric — the level is pinned at ceiling.

- **[F6a] Torrens B withholds to floor-only in ~60–65% of its CLEANEST competitive intervals — the baseline rate that makes raw withholding counts uninformative.** `outputs/withhold_opportunity/stage1b_diagnostics.md`, `stage1_baseline_summary.csv` (`wo_stage1_baseline.R`; 1.89M in-force bid rows, 36 months). In *undirected, non-pivotal, available* intervals — the regime with the weakest strategic incentive — the gas-steam units offer only their $0 floor tranche (cheap capacity ≤$300 below 100 MW): **TORRB2 64.8%, TORRB3 61.5%, TORRB4 59.7%** (median cheap ≈60 MW of ~200 MAXAVAIL). Their competitive offer is bimodal (floor mode ≈60 MW / full-cheap mode ≈200 MW; empirical trough ≈130–170 MW). CCGT/cogen differ sharply: PPCCGT 5.0%, OSB-AG 3.2% floor-only. **Rules in:** withholding the cheap tranche is Torrens B's *ordinary* competitive conduct, not a special response — so on any "opportunity" subset a high withheld rate barely exceeds this baseline and cannot by itself identify directions-seeking (per the revealed-preference guardrails). **Consequence:** identification of a directions motive must rest on d_t *specificity* (does withholding sort on the directed price, and only on opportunity intervals vs a matched comparison set), not on the withholding rate.

- **[F7] Raw withheld-share-vs-d_t slopes are sign-heterogeneous and confounded.** `Cut3_slopes.csv`, `Cut3_withheld_vs_dt.png`. Per-unit OLS slope of mean withheld_share on d_t (per $100): PPCCGT +0.060, TORRB2 +0.028, OSB-AG +0.012, then negative for TORRB3 (−0.004), DRYCGT (−0.005 to −0.012), BARKIPS1 (−0.026), QPS5 (−0.033), TORRB4 (−0.039), MINTARO (−0.046). R² ≤ 0.24. **Rules in:** nothing on its own — the raw relationship is contaminated because d_t and SRMC both spiked in 2022 (high SRMC mechanically lowers the share priced above it). Motivates the conditional design [F8].

- **[F8] Conditional on cost+price environment, withheld_share rises with d_t (the sign reversal).** `Cut5_regression.csv`, `Cut5_spot_robustness.csv`, `Cut5_SRMC_controlled.png`, `Cut5_SRMC_spot_controlled.png`. Outcome = withheld_share (MW priced above SRMC_marginal / MAXAVAIL); unit FE; d_t standardised.
  - Unit-month (n=361, het-robust SE): no SRMC control β = **−0.0019** (t −0.28) → +SRMC β = **+0.0198** (t 2.79).
  - Interval-level (n=2,298,025, clustered by month=35): FE-only β = −0.0048 (t −0.54) → +SRMC β = **+0.0128** (t 2.12) → +SRMC+spot(5-min native) β = +0.0128 (t 2.09) → +SRMC+spot(1-day lag) β = +0.0127 (t 2.04).
  **Rules in:** a positive, significant within-unit response of withholding to d_t once the 2022 price regime is partialled out, ≈ **+2 pp of MAXAVAIL per SD of d_t** (unit-month). Robust to spot at native resolution. **Caveat:** identified off 35 monthly d_t values; analytic clustering on 35 clusters → wild-cluster bootstrap needed for honest inference.

- **[F9] Within-day, withheld_share rises with instantaneous rent.** `Cut6_regression.csv`, `Cut6_within_day_rent.png`. withheld_share ~ (d_t − spot), unit×month FE, clustered by DUID: β = **1.4e-5** (se 3e-6, t 4.52, n=2,298,025). Identified purely off within-unit-month spot variation (d_t fixed in month). Magnitude ≈ 0.14 pp per $100 of rent. **Rules in:** real-time responsiveness of the offer stack to rent, the cleanest-FE corroboration of [F8]. **Caveat:** only 11–12 DUID clusters → wild-cluster bootstrap essential.

- **[F10] Response heterogeneity does not line up with margin size.** `Heterogeneity_slopes.csv`, `Heterogeneity_slopes.png`. Per-unit slope (withheld_share ~ d_t std) vs mean (d_t − SRMC): PPCCGT +0.032 (margin $133), TORRB2 +0.022 ($80), TORRB3 +0.009 ($90), then ≤0 for OSB-AG, TORRB4 (−0.011), DRYCGT, BARKIPS1 (−0.015), QPS5 (−0.018), MINTARO (−0.043). **Rules in:** the positive response concentrates in CCGT (PPCCGT) and two Torrens B units; **rules out** a simple "larger rent → larger response" story — the slope is not monotone in margin. Within Torrens Island B the sign is mixed (TORRB2/3 positive, TORRB4 negative).

## D. Exogeneity of AEMO direction intensity

- **[F11] Direction volume is only weakly correlated with the rent.** `Margin_vs_DirectionVolume.png`, `Margin_vs_DirectionVolume_timeseries.png`. Monthly fleet totals: corr(mean margin, directed unit-hours) = **0.167**; OLS slope 0.58 unit-hours per $/MWh. Margin peaks early 2023 ($199–203); direction volume peaks late 2024 (Oct-2024 = 1,029 unit-hours) — the two series' peaks are offset. Apr–May 2022 had **negative** margin yet positive direction volume. **Rules in:** AEMO direction frequency is driven by system-security need, not generator rent — supports treating direction *incidence* as exogenous to the rent. **Rules out:** a story where AEMO mechanically directs more when the prize is large.

- **[F12] Cross-unit treatment variation exists; frequency is AEMO-driven.** `outputs/descriptives/descriptive_readout.md`, `Cut1_treatment_variation.png`. 13 directed SA1 DUIDs over the window; 146,393 Synchronise vs 227,117 Remain intervals. Event counts concentrate on Torrens B (TORRB3 353, TORRB4 287, TORRB2 266, TORRB1 55), then MINTARO 236, PPCCGT 212. Monthly direction-frequency-vs-d_t corr = −0.078. **Rules in:** sufficient cross-unit variation for FE designs; the Synchronise/Remain split is the withhold-then-directed margin vs the keep-running placebo.

## E. System-strength pivotality and strategic withholding

Source: `outputs/descriptives_v3/pivotality_readout.md`; built from AEMO's minimum synchronous generator combinations (`sa_minimum_generator_combinations.csv`) + DISPATCHLOAD online status (all 36 months, `extract_dispatchload.R`). Pivotal_t(s) = 1 iff removing station s makes every applicable acceptable combination infeasible. **Realised** (`piv_*`) drops s from its own online set (endogenous); **ex-ante** (`pex_*`) tests essentiality given *rivals' availability only* (exogenous to s's own withholding).

- **[F13] Pivotality is concentrated and validated against directions.** `pivotality_panel.rds`. Realised-pivotal share of intervals: Torrens Island B **42.4%**, Pelican Point 15.3%, Mintaro 11.8%, Quarantine 1.6%, others ~0. Ex-ante: TIB 1.3%, all others ~0 (peakers sit ~100% available, so strict essentiality is rare). Validation: P(TIB pivotal | TIB directed) = **0.65** vs 0.44 baseline; P(Pelican pivotal | directed) = **0.35** vs 0.17 (2.1×). **Rules in:** the measure tracks genuine security need — AEMO directs units 1.5–2× more when they are pivotal; TIB is the system-strength backbone. **Rules out:** pivotality being noise unrelated to directions.

- **[F14] Pivotal units withhold ~11–14 pp more — robust to endogeneity.** `Pivotality_level_effects.csv`, `Pivotality_withholding_bars.png`. Outcome `withheld_share`; unit FE; +SRMC+spot; clustered by month. Realised pivotal: **+0.128** (t 11.8) all, **+0.111** (t 9.4) undirected-only. **Ex-ante pivotal: +0.140 (t 3.0) undirected** — larger than realised, significant despite 1.3% prevalence. Continuous exogenous driver (non-sync penetration, weather-driven): **+0.0054 per +100 MW** (t 8.6). Within pivotal-capable units, undirected: **TORRB3 70%→88%** withheld when pivotal; TIB(4) +0.175 (t 6.8); PPCCGT 60%→74% (+0.134, t 3.8); Mintaro ns. **Rules in:** units withhold more precisely when system-strength-essential *and* market-facing — the direct test of the RQ. The ex-ante and non-sync results defuse the "directed→online→pivotal→offers-high" mechanical channel: units made pivotal by forces outside their control still withhold ~14 pp more. **Rules out:** the level effect being a pure artifact of the direction itself.

- **[F14a] The recovered offer curves show withholding on BOTH margins, triggered by pivotality and building in the run-up.** `outputs/descriptives_v3/supply_curves/` (`04_market_power/supply_curves_by_regime.R`, `f4_supply_curves.R`) and `outputs/direction_rebid/f5_runup_supply_curves*` (`05_directions/f5_runup_supply_curves.R`). Each unit's inverse supply curve = offer price/SRMC (log) at each cumulative-capacity quantile, cumulative offer capped at MAXAVAIL, median across months. **(i) 2×2 direction×pivotality:** for Torrens B the undirected-non-pivotal curve has a genuine cheap tranche (30–50% of capacity at 2–5× SRMC); once the unit is pivotal OR directed the tranche vanishes and the whole curve sits at ~130× SRMC. When **pivotal**, TORRB `avail_frac` collapses **1.0 → 0.20** (registered) *and* the available remainder is priced ~at the cap — a simultaneous quantity + price withdrawal; PPCCGT curves truncate early (cap reached at ~50% vs ~85% undirected). Direction adds little beyond pivotality (UP ≈ DP curves), consistent with [F14]. **(ii) F4 nested cuts (all / directed / directed-&-pivotal):** the curve sits far above SRMC (=1) in every cut and rises further when directed — the rent of [F4] visualised. **(iii) F5 run-up — composition-corrected.** The naive pooled-by-lead-bin supply curve (`f5_runup_supply_curves.R`) *appeared* to show the cheap tranche withdrawn as issue τ nears, but this is largely a **composition artifact**: the near-issue lead bins are populated by only 18–32 of ~130 episodes — a self-selected high-withholding subset — not the same episodes as the far bins (diagnosis: distinct-episode counts per lead bin). The **composition-proof within-episode trajectory** (`f5b_within_episode_runup.R`: carry each episode's own bid forward to a common event-time grid, fixed set = **100% of episodes**) shows **no pre-issue cheap-tranche withdrawal** — for Torrens B the cheap tranche is a flat ~5% of registered cap from −24h through issue (point estimates slightly *positive*, +1–2pp; CIs straddle 0), i.e. withholding is a **standing posture [F6]**, already ~95% in place a day before issue, not assembled in the run-up. Quantity margin is **heterogeneous**: Torrens B *raise* MAXAVAIL toward a Synchronise (+6–8%, preparing to come online) while peakers Mintaro/QPS5 *cut* it (−15%/−6%). **Rules in:** cross-sectional dual-margin withholding keyed to pivotality (the 2×2, well identified). **Rules out:** an *unconditional* pre-issue run-up in the cheap tranche — that pooled-bin impression does not survive holding the episode set fixed. Consistent with [F16], whose withdrawal result is *conditional on pivotal share*; the natural next step is a within-episode × pivotality split. **Caveat:** `avail_frac` in pivotal intervals blends deliberate withdrawal with any genuine derating; pivotal intervals are a selected subsample.

- **[F15] Pivotality state dominates prize size.** Same source. The d_t × pivotal interaction is insignificant (`Pivotality_interaction.csv`); the d_t slope (~+0.013) is a second-order margin once pivotality is in. **Rules in:** "am I needed?" (pivotality state, +13 pp) drives withholding far more than "how big is the rent?" (d_t slope, ~2 pp/SD) — sharpens the mechanism from the Cut 5 slope story.

- **[F16] Pivotal units actively rebid on two withholding levers.** `rebid_pivotality_daily.csv`, `Rebid_vs_pivotality.png`. Rebid intensity (distinct `OFFERDATETIME` versions/day; mean 14.5, PPCCGT up to 51) is **not** higher when pivotal (−2.5/day undirected, t −4.2). But the *direction* of rebidding is: (a) **quantity** — interval-fixed intraday MAXAVAIL withdrawn on realised pivotal share = **+40.2 MW, undirected-only** (t 5.3; ≈20% of a TIB unit); (b) **price** — above-SRMC capacity share, last version minus first = **+9.3 pp** realised (t 6.3), +4.2 pp undirected (t 2.4), **+18.3 pp ex-ante** (t 5.3), +0.57 pp per +100 MW non-sync (t 8.1). **Rules in:** withholding runs on **both** the quantity (pull MAXAVAIL) and price (push MW above SRMC) margins, actively via intraday rebids; the price-escalation result survives undirected-only and is *strongest under the exogenous ex-ante measure* — dynamic active conduct, not a mechanical direction artifact. **Rules out:** pivotal units merely churning bids (they churn fewer, but net-withhold on both margins).

- **[F17] Why non-pivotal units are directed: two drivers verified, one construct-logical but not empirically separable.** `direction_data/parsed/direction_events.csv` (`reason` field, 1,637 SA1 unit-events), `reason_pivotality.R`, `perdirection_hitrate_*.csv`, `perinterval_vs_perdirection.csv`. The per-interval P(pivotal | directed) in [F13] (TIB 0.65, Pelican 0.35) is computed against **all-cause** directions, so ~35–65% of directed intervals look non-pivotal. Three forces were tested; the data verdict on each:
  1. **Spell dilution — VERIFIED, the largest driver.** Directions are multi-hour spells (median **9.0 h**, mean **18.7 h**, max 241 h); pivotality is a 5-min flag. Per-direction (event-level) hit rates vs the per-interval rate, 2022–24 (`perinterval_vs_perdirection.csv`): TIB per-interval 0.648 → **per-direction hit-any 0.703**, but **mean fraction of the spell pivotal = 0.40** (hit-at-issue 0.53). So a typical TIB direction touches a pivotal interval 70% of the time yet is pivotal only ~40% of its duration — ~60% of a directed spell's intervals are mechanically non-pivotal. Pelican 0.40, Mintaro 0.24, QPS5 0.13 fraction pivotal. This explains most non-pivotal directed *intervals*.
  2. **Individual vs collective pivotality — VERIFIED.** The measure flags s pivotal only if removing *s alone* breaks every combination. Substitutable peakers are directed yet rarely *individually* essential: per-direction hit-any QPS5 0.31, Mintaro 0.49, BIPS 0.00, Osborne 0.07; ex-ante ≈0 for all. AEMO picks one unit from a feasible set → it lands on individually-non-pivotal units by construction. Non-pivotal = not *uniquely* needed, not not-needed.
  3. **Reason-construct mismatch — logically valid, NOT empirically demonstrable here.** Pivotality encodes only the **system-strength** standard (min synchronous-generator combinations); directions also cover voltage/inertia/network. Descriptively the directed set is provably a superset: the `reason` label **"System strength" appears only in 2021**; across **202201–202412 every direction is generic "System security"** (2022: 308, 2023: 441, 2024: 153 for pivotal-capable stations), with a 2024 **"System security - voltage"** subcategory (151). BUT the cross-year test to *quantify* this fails: extending pivotality to 2021 (`reason_pivotality.R`, 2021 DISPATCHLOAD extracted) gives P(pivotal | 2021 strength directions) = **0.000** — an artifact, because 2021 pivotality is ≈0 (Jan–Oct = 0%) from the 4-syncon assumption (syncons commissioned *through* 2021) **and** genuinely low 2021 non-sync penetration (system strength rarely bound pre-renewable-surge). And the one clean in-sample split contradicts the naive prediction: 2024 **voltage** directions are *more* pivotal, not less (LPM +0.10 interval t 4.0; +0.22 per-direction t 5.5) — reason and pivotality are positively correlated through the unit/state, so the reason field cannot separate strength from non-strength need in this sample. **Conclusion:** construct mismatch is real in principle but unquantified; drivers 1–2 carry the explanation.
  **Rules in:** non-pivotal-but-directed is expected — driven by sticky multi-hour spells (driver 1) and substitutable units AEMO selects among (driver 2), both verified. [F13]'s 0.65 is a per-interval lower bound; the per-direction hit-any is 0.70 for TIB. **Rules out:** treating P(pivotal|directed)<1 as evidence the measure is broken; and the naive "non-pivotal = directed-for-voltage/non-strength" story (driver 3), which the data does not support. **Does not touch** [F14]–[F16], which condition on the pivotal *state* (incl. exogenous ex-ante) — unaffected by all three. **Institutional grounding** (AEMO/AEMC sources + data cross-checks) in `why_directed_nonpivotal.md`: six reasons — minimum-combination not named-unit; forward-looking secure envelope; the **islanding contingency** (our measure excludes the 43 `risk_island` combinations — testable, highest-value fix); un-encoded **inertia/voltage** services; operational margin; spell lumpiness.

- **[F18] N-1 contingency pivotality + decomposition of the directed-but-non-pivotal mass.** `pivotality.R` (extended), `pivotality_decomposition.R`, `pivotal_shares_base_n1_exante.csv`, `directed_nonpivotal_waterfall.csv`. Added an **N-1** variant (base `piv` / ex-ante `pex` unchanged): per interval, remove the single largest online synchronous unit, then test each station's pivotality on the post-contingency fleet (`piv_n1_*`), storing `on_*` counts and a `short_n1` flag.
  - **`short_n1 = 46.6%`** of intervals (vs base `short` = 0.3%): SA cannot survive loss of its largest online synchronous unit without further commitment **nearly half the time** — a first-order, quantified reason AEMO directs beyond the base minimum.
  - **Pivotality is monotone** — removing the largest unit can only make more units pivotal (verified: 0 violations of base⟹N-1 on N-1-secure intervals). Compared on the same denominator (N-1-secure intervals), **N-1 ≫ base**: TIB 33.4% vs 2.7%; Pelican 19.5% vs 0.1%. The literal `piv_n1` = `short_n1 + clean` (TIB 64.4 = 46.6 + 17.8); its ~47% floor across all stations (incl. never-pivotal BIPS/Snapper) is the trivial-flag region where `short_n1` holds. A "clean N-1 as share of *all* intervals" (TIB 17.8%) looks below base only because it zeroes the `short_n1` half where base pivotality is concentrated (41 of TIB's 42 base-pivotal pp are on `short_n1` intervals) — **not** a decrease in pivotality.
  - **Decomposition waterfall** of the 76,489 directed & base-non-pivotal interval-stations (residual after each cut): drop Remain → 66.7%; drop voltage reason → 58.4%; drop `short_n1` (directed to restore N-1 security) → 41.2%; drop clean N-1-pivotal → 25.3%; drop near-margin (≤2 combos survive) → **20.5%**. So **~80% is explained**; the final 20.5% residual (Mintaro 8,279 + TIB 5,391 dominate) is "comfortably redundant" directions = the substitutable-unit / operational-discretion case ([F17] driver 2; `why_directed_nonpivotal.md` reasons 1,5). **Rules in:** the credible-contingency (N-1) and collective-substitutability reasons together account for the large majority of non-pivotal directions; the genuinely-puzzling residual is one-fifth and concentrated in peakers AEMO picks among. **Caveat:** N-1 uses the same `system_normal`, 4-syncon, MW-engine assumptions as base; the islanding (`risk_island`) combinations remain excluded.

- **[F19] Depth-of-pivotality (continuous distance-to-essential) + a DISPATCHLOAD dedup fix.** `pivotality.R` (extended: `depth_ex_*`, `depth_rl_*`, exact memoized solver), `depth_report.R`, `depth_distribution_{exante,realised}.csv`, `depth_by_nonsync_tier.csv`. **k_i** = minimum number of rival units to remove (unit i excluded) before the fleet satisfies no applicable combination; **k=0 ⟺ pivotal**. Two versions paralleling `pex`/`piv`: **depth_ex** (primary, over rivals' *available* fleet — exogenous) and **depth_rl** (over rivals' *online* fleet). Solver validated against brute force (400 random states, 0 failures) and the built-in **regression test passes on all 8 stations × 36 months: `depth_ex==0` reproduces `pex` exactly** (stops the run otherwise).
  - **Dedup bug fixed:** some early months (e.g. 202201) carried 4× duplicate DISPATCHLOAD rows, inflating unit counts (TIB online count up to 12 vs physical 4) — harmless for the binary flag (thresholds ≤4) but fatal for depth. Deduped by `(SETTLEMENTDATE, DUID)` keeping max `TOTALCLEARED`. **Base `piv`/`pex` unchanged** (TIB 42.4%, pex 1.3% — verified identical post-fix).
  - **Ex-ante depth (available rivals, primary):** mean k — TIB **3.1** (median 3), Pelican 3.6, QPS5 3.6, Mintaro **4.3** (deepest = most redundant). TIB is the shallowest = structurally closest to essential, consistent with it being the system-strength backbone. (k=0 share = `pex`, tiny.)
  - **Realised depth (online rivals):** much shallower — TIB mean **1.0** (median 1), Pelican 1.3, Mintaro 2.0. The online fleet sits ~2 units closer to the security edge than the available fleet — the structural footprint of withholding (units available but not online). `depth_rl==0` (TIB 48.3%) is *station-level* online pivotality (all of i removed), distinct from the one-unit base `piv` (42.4%); documented, not asserted equal.
  - **Crosstab depth × non-sync tier:** ex-ante depth **falls monotonically as non-sync rises** — TIB 3.3 (<1300 MW) → 1.7 (1900–2100 MW); pivotal share 0.9% → 7.0%. As renewables crowd out synchronous headroom, TIB moves continuously toward essential — the smooth, continuous form of the tightness mechanism behind [F14]/[F15] (the depth measure replaces the binary pivotal flag's knife-edge with a graded distance-to-essential, a better RHS variable for the bidding regressions).
