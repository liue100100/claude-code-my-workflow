# Direction events: ex-ante depth and pre-issue rebid run-up

Generated by `00_episodes.R`, `A_depth_eventstudy.R`, `B_rebid_runup.R`
(driver: `run_direction_rebid.R`). All inputs reuse the existing bid cache,
pivotality panel, treatment panel, and SRMC parameters.

## Episodes

1,492 direction episodes on the 12 directed SA focal-station DUIDs
(730 Synchronise / 762 Remain), each kept per-DUID with three timestamps:
issue τ, effective s, cancellation c, and lead = s − τ. 37 episodes were issued
at or after their effective time and carry no pre-issue window. Median lead is
15 h (Synchronise) and 9 h (Remain); median directed duration is 8.2 h and 14.5 h.

## Analysis A — ex-ante depth around the start s

Ex-ante depth is the own-status-invariant count of available rivals
(`depth_ex_<station>`); lower means more pivotal. Measured in event time over
e ∈ [−12, +24] five-minute intervals centred on the first directed interval, and
compared at onset (first hour), tail (last hour before c), and a matched
non-directed counterfactual (same station × hour × month × weekend).

| instruction | onset | tail | matched | onset vs matched | onset vs tail |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Synchronise | 3.14 | 3.20 | 3.94 | p ≈ 9e−54 | p = 0.20 |
| Remain | 2.44 | 2.89 | 3.36 | p ≈ 3e−42 | p = 8e−10 |

Directions land when the unit is markedly more pivotal than its own typical
non-directed state at the same hour and month: onset depth is about 0.8–0.9
rivals below the matched baseline for both instruction types. For Synchronise the
unit stays equally pivotal through the episode (onset ≈ tail); for Remain it
becomes more redundant by the tail.

Files: `A_depth_profile.{csv,png}`, `A_onset_tail_matched.csv`, `A_episode_depth.rds`.

## Analysis B — pre-issue rebid run-up (Δ = OFFERDATETIME − τ)

For every episode we collect all `BIDOFFERPERIOD` versions whose target interval
g lies in the directed window, indexed by Δ = submission − issue. Δ<0 is the
pre-issue run-up; Δ>0 is contaminated by the direction itself and is plotted but
never interpreted. Two target sets are run side by side: the whole window
g ∈ [s,c] and the onset only g ∈ [s, s+1h]. Run-up is measured from the earliest
pre-issue version (baseline) to the last pre-issue version. 1,050 episodes carry
at least one bid version inside the 2022–2024 cache; 669 Synchronise / 380 Remain
episodes have a usable pre-issue window and an onset-depth measure.

Mean pre-issue change (Δ<0):

| scope | instruction | mean Δ-versions | ΔMAXAVAIL (MW) | Δ above-SRMC share |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| onset | Synchronise | 6.0 | +12.3 | +0.029 |
| onset | Remain | 6.2 | +8.2 | +0.106 |
| whole | Synchronise | 9.1 | +9.8 | +0.025 |
| whole | Remain | 9.2 | +8.9 | +0.078 |

(ΔMAXAVAIL reported as last − baseline; positive = capacity *raised* over the
pre-issue window. Above-SRMC share rises pre-issue for both.)

**Headline (run-up × ex-ante depth × post-2023-07 exit), `runup_abovesrmc`:**
For Synchronise, the pre-issue shift of capacity above SRMC is significantly
*larger the more pivotal the unit is* — the `depth_onset` coefficient is negative
and significant (−0.019**, whole; −0.023***, onset). The Remain placebo shows no
such depth gradient (−0.000, −0.010, both n.s.). The whole-window and onset
definitions give the same picture, with the onset price-shift channel slightly
stronger. The capacity (`runup_withhold`) channel is weaker and noisier: more
pivotal Synchronise units withdraw relatively more capacity (depth coefficient
−9.2*, onset), but the level effect is dominated by units bringing capacity online.

The d_t (post-2023-07 exit) main effect and interaction are significant only in
the Remain placebo, not the Synchronise headline.

Files: `B_versions.rds`, `B_trace.{csv}`, `B_trace_maxavail.png`,
`B_trace_abovesrmc.png`, `B_runup_metrics.csv`, `B_runup_regression.txt`.
