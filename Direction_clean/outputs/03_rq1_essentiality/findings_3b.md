## (A) Symmetric fragility: leave-one-month-out paths for BOTH headline unit results
Full paths in `stage3b_lomo_path.csv` / `.png` (36 re-estimates per unit x outcome, M3
specification). Months whose removal flips the sign or moves p across the 0.05 or 0.10 threshold:

- **Pelican Point** (+0.184 base, p=0.070 fixed-$300): 3 flagged months (202203, 202310, 202403 on
  both definitions) -- and one of them is disqualifying: **dropping October 2023 alone FLIPS the
  sign** (+0.184 -> -0.083 fixed; +0.128 -> -0.105 cost-indexed). The entire positive Pelican Point
  response is carried by a single month. (Dropping 202203 or 202403 pushes the coefficient the
  other way, across the 0.05 line -- unstable in both directions.)
- **Torrens** (-0.076 base, p=0.039 cost-indexed): 9 flagged months -- but every flag is a
  p-threshold crossing in the 0.03-0.15 band, and **the coefficient stays negative in all 72
  re-estimates**. No sign flips. The flags are borderline-significance wobble, not sign
  instability.

The raw flag-counts (9 vs 3) invert the honest ranking: read qualitatively, Torrens is a stable,
consistently negative, borderline-significant response; **Pelican Point's positive response is a
one-month artifact and should not be promoted in the write-up as a substantive finding** -- at
most a footnote that its sign is unstable. The 'essential CCGT wants to run' interpretation
offered in the Stage-3 findings does not survive this diagnostic and is withdrawn.

## (B) Three-way June-2022 split (segments from AEMO's own per-interval flags, not assumed dates)
- Suspension window: **2022-06-15 14:10:00 -> 2022-06-24 14:00:00** (contiguity verified: zero unflagged intervals inside).
- Pre-suspension June includes an administered-price (APC) sub-period from 2022-06-05 18:05 --
  those days are not ordinary pricing either; APC interval counts reported per segment below.

Segment denominators (Torrens; unit-rows = interval x unit):
- pre_suspension: 4201 intervals (12603 unit-rows), 957 essential unit-rows (319 essential intervals), 395 APC intervals
- suspension_window: 2591 intervals (7773 unit-rows), 1365 essential unit-rows (455 essential intervals), 514 APC intervals
- post_suspension: 1848 intervals (5544 unit-rows), 0 essential unit-rows (0 essential intervals), 0 APC intervals
Torrens essential intervals in the whole 36-month sample: 4083.

**Reading -- the Torrens result is NOT suspension-era pricing conduct.** Dropping the suspension
window alone leaves the coefficient essentially intact (-0.068, p=0.054 cost-indexed; the fixed
definition actually strengthens, -0.043, p=0.086): pre-suspension June essential intervals plus
the rest of the sample sustain the negative response without any suspension-window data.
Symmetrically, dropping the pre-suspension fortnight alone also leaves it intact (-0.058,
p=0.045). Only removing BOTH segments -- all of June, 2,322 of 12,249 essential unit-rows (19%),
with zero essential rows post-suspension -- degrades it to p=0.13. So the earlier Stage-3 caveat
('the significance leans on June 2022') is refined: the result leans on June's essential-interval
MASS, not on suspension-era administered-price conduct specifically. That is a statistical-power
dependence, not a confounding story -- a materially better position for the write-up, stated with
the denominators above. Residual caveat: the pre-suspension segment itself contains the APC
sub-period (395 of its 4,201 intervals), so 'ordinary pricing' does not describe all of
pre-suspension June either.
