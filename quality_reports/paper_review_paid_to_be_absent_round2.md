## Summary of the Revision

The revision is substantial and, more importantly, honest. The authors ran the four diagnostics round 1 asked for — the month-grain plot (Fig 6), the fuel horse race, the roll-off/lag-wedge timing contrast, and the placebo (found structurally infeasible, with the corrected feasibility gate and its own coding error reported rather than hidden) — and then recalibrated the paper's causal language to what the evidence supports: the abstract now says withholding "co-varies with" the payment and the cross-month gradient is "consistent with payment-seeking rather than proof of it." The new floor-reach decomposition (§6.3, T5) is the revision's best addition: it relocates the dose response onto exactly the margin the mechanism pays (the self-commitment/eligibility choice, −8.6 pp per \$100/MWh, WCB p=.003, randomization inference p=.046), shows the intensive margin is dead, and thereby resolves the round-1 estimand tension between §6 and §7 — a standing posture whose *coverage* tracks the payment across months is now the stated estimand (§5.4), and the month-grain aggregate (−0.096 per \$100/MWh, HC1 p=.001, 19 months) confirms the interval result survives aggregation to the grain a skeptic trusts. Each test's interpretation was committed in the project record before estimation, at named commits, and the adjudications in the findings files apply the committed readings without smoothing — including the one that went against the paper (the lag-wedge primary contrast at WCB p=.089 triggered the "soften to consistent-with" decision rule, and the softening was carried through abstract, intro, §6, and §8).

Two round-1 majors remain genuinely open. The d_t feedback loop (MC2) — the focal units' own withholding sits in the spot tail that the trailing 90th percentile samples — is still nowhere acknowledged in the manuscript; this is now the single most exposed flank. And the inference upgrade is half-done: randomization inference (null-imposed by construction) was added and passes at p=.046, but the null-imposed wild bootstrap proper is explicitly deferred to "a pre-submission upgrade," and the RI margin is thin. The welfare re-benchmarking (RO3) got the right scope paragraphs (§9.1: the 20:1 figure compares payment rates, not welfare; the retirement counterfactual is named; the syncons are the revealed benchmark) but not the requested numbers (VOLL-valued security, annualized syncon cost). Literature positioning is transformed (7+ new entries, with real engagement of Reguant's commitment-cost account and Hirth–Schlecht's inc-dec frame in §1), though the vertical-position pair (Bushnell–Mansur–Saravia; Ito–Reguant) and NEM academic scholarship are still absent — which matters because they back the still-partial portfolio concern (MC5).

**Recommendation: MINOR REVISION.** The causal centerpiece is now stated at the strength the evidence supports, and the remaining items are executable without new identification work.

## 2. New Concerns Introduced by the Revision

- **N1 (consistency). The 12,513 essential-row count vs the base sample.** §5.4 and §3.3 describe the matched panel as "140,259 rows; 12,513 essential," but the analysis record (findings_test1) notes the base-filtered sample of 140,259 rows contains 11,120 essential rows — 12,513 includes the June-2022 suspension-window mass that the base sample excludes. The manuscript pairs the base-sample row count with the all-treatments essential count. One clarifying parenthetical (or report both counts) prevents a referee from finding the discrepancy the way this reviewer did.
- **N2 (interpretation). The positive essential main effect is undiscussed.** T2 now reports β = +0.089 (p<0.10): at low d_t the essential-vs-matched gap in the cheap share is *positive* — the units are more present when essential and the prize is small — and Fig 6 shows the same crossover (positive gaps below ≈ \$175, deepening negative gaps above). This is arguably *additional* evidence for payment-seeking (presence when essentiality pays little, absence when it pays a lot) and is currently left for the reader to notice. One or two sentences in §6.2/§6.3 would both preempt the "why is your main effect the wrong sign?" question and strengthen the story.
- **N3 (consistency). Two leftover "preregistered" instances** (§1 line ~79; Appendix A line ~8) contradict the deliberate global rewording — see mc8. Trivial fix, but as it stands the paper uses both vocabularies.
- **N4 (risk, not error). The inference chain now leans on RI p=.046.** The abstract quotes it; the null-imposed WCB is promised pre-submission. If that upgrade lands above 0.05 on the eligibility margin, the abstract's inferential sentence needs rewriting. Run it before submission, not after a data editor does.
- **N5 (internal consistency). §5.5's inference footnote still asserts the unrestricted-vs-null-imposed difference "is second-order at 36 clusters"** while §8.4 now (correctly) concedes the unrestricted variant "can be liberal with few unbalanced clusters" and supplies RI for exactly that reason. Delete the second-order sentence from the footnote; §8.4's posture is the right one.
- **N6 (cosmetic). T5's table note has math split across raw line breaks** in the source ("base $ / −0.019 / $, $p= / 0.55 / $") — renders, but will annoy a copyeditor; and "19 essential-bearing months" (Fig 6, ≥30-row filter) vs "21 effective month-clusters" (§5.5) deserves the one-clause reconciliation the Fig 6 caption only half provides.

None of these is blocking; N1/N3/N5 are consistency cleanups, N2 is an opportunity, N4 is the one genuine pre-submission risk.

## 4. Updated Recommendation

**MINOR REVISION.**

The round-1 verdict said the identification claim outran the evidence. It no longer does: the requested diagnostics were run, the one that came back marginal was reported at its marginal value, and the paper's causal language was resized accordingly — "consistent with payment-seeking," with the decisive, fully-powered result (the eligibility-margin dose response, confirmed at the month grain and under randomization inference) now carrying the headline on exactly the margin the mechanism pays. The descriptive, mechanism, and design-forensics contributions were already publishable; the causal architecture is now defensible as stated.

What remains is a bounded list, none of it requiring new identification:

1. **MC2 (the only untouched major):** one paragraph acknowledging the withholding → spot-tail → d_t loop, plus a bounding calculation of the units' plausible pass-through to the trailing 90th percentile, and a sentence reading θ as a reduced-form equilibrium gradient under feedback. (If the pass-through bound is small, this paragraph *strengthens* the paper.)
2. **Inference (MC6/N4/N5):** run the null-imposed WCB before submission (Rtools, as the record already plans); add the leave-three-top-months-out estimate; delete the "second-order" sentence from §5.5's footnote.
3. **MC5:** two sentences describing AGL's SA portfolio/retail position; cite BMS (2008) and Ito–Reguant (2016); add the one free sentence noting the timing wedge also disciplines the portfolio channel.
4. **MC7/RO3:** the two back-of-envelope numbers (annualized syncon cost per MW-of-presence; directed-energy value at VOLL) and the caveat clause in the abstract's 20:1 sentence.
5. **Mechanical sweep:** the six unaddressed minors (T1 Panel B Ns and M1–M3 for Torrens; Fig 1 sign shading; Fig 2 sentence or panel; Fig 3 outage note; the 0.95-factor sentence; §2 institutional paragraph on suspension/APC and "remain" compensation), the two leftover "preregistered" strings (N3), the essential-count parenthetical (N1), and the positive-main-effect discussion (N2).

Items 2's WCB run and item 1 are the only ones with any capacity to change a conclusion; everything else is presentation. Conditional on those landing where the current record suggests they will, this paper is publishable at a good field journal.
