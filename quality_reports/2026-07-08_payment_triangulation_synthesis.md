## What stands

1. **The conduct is commercially explicable at every margin where incentives are separately
   identified.** Running at floor is unprofitable on 82% of clean unit-days once start costs
   are priced; the on/off choice cliffs at the commercial break-even and slides flat through
   the option line; the scenario needed for classic gaming — profitable running declined for a
   better-paying option — occurred on 13 Torrens unit-days in three years (< $1M against the
   $141.9M transfer).
2. **The payment is largely infra-marginal — the mechanism's cost is a design problem, not a
   gaming problem.** Because commercial logic already produces the absence, the gross,
   backward-looking, make-whole payment transfers rents without needing any behavioral
   response: $141.9M (DCP alone; 3.15.7B top-ups add ~17–20% in normal months and ~100% where
   the cost floor binds) for output worth $7.2M at spot, with 2023 paying 2022 crisis rates. No
   conduct remedy addresses this; only the formula does.
3. **Conduct does track exposure.** Floor-reach falls ~40 pp as the rivals-only direction
   propensity moves 0 → 1 (robust to the day-ahead timing-immunized π). The committed fleet is
   below the minimum-synchronous standard 49.4% of all half-hours while declared availability
   covers it 98.6% — the direction mechanism substitutes for a commitment product the
   energy-only market does not sell, and the market's ability to procure commitment collapses
   exactly when the system needs it.
4. **One payment-adjacent fact survives, bounded:** the realized-essential × gross-d_t gradient
   (−8.6 pp per $100, WCB p < 0.001, RI p = 0.046). Today's work confined its interpretation —
   inseparable from fuel-cost co-movement under make-whole, non-generalizing to every ex-ante
   margin — without overturning it. The manuscript's registered "consistent with
   payment-seeking rather than proof" is exactly calibrated: retain, neither harden nor delete.
